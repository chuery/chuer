Vectors and Virtues: Mackie’s Anti-realist Arguments and Heumer’s Potential Realist Responses

This is my case for acting based on my moral intuitions.
Emotion motivates my moral intuition; I construct my decision-making processes from that runway of foundational principles. Is moral intuition a fair principle to act upon? I align with Huemer’s statement that moral intuition is like mathematical knowledge because it is a form of rational intuition—a reliable and competent faculty for acquiring knowledge. Ethical knowledge is relevantly similar to mathematical knowledge in its acquisition process. Mathematical truths can come from intuition because there is some knowledge that is self-evident, if difficult to formally prove, this also works for the domain of ethics. In both cases, intuition allows us to access truths that are objective without empirical derivation. Thus, ethical intuition shares the same epistemic merits as mathematical intuition insofar as they both employ rationalistic intuition. With that, I hesitate to say that everyone has a moral responsibility to the world, but I feel that I do. Then, the sheer scope and magnitude of time I will spend on my career makes it the biggest moral decision I will make in this life. Because of this, an important goal of mine for my college years is to have formulated certainty in the direction that I am taking so that post-grad I can be sure I am making intentional decisions based on my moral principles and my cognizance of death. Being intentional does not mean that I have defined metrics and granular goals for every step that I take, but rather that each step is in the direction of something like a life’s work.
